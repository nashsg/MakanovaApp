🧭 The Makanova 4-Pillar Blueprint

 🧬 Pillar 1: Asian Genetics (The Base Engine)

* The Reality: Due to genetic body composition, Asians store more visceral fat around their organs. This causes high risks for Type-2 diabetes and heart issues at a much lower weight than Westerners.
* What Makanova Does: The onboarding setup throws out Western health math. It uses the official WHO Asian BMI thresholds (where "overweight" begins at 23 instead of 25) and tracks waist-to-hip measurements to accurately flag hidden metabolic risks.

🍜 Pillar 2: Asian Diets (The Meal Tracker)

* The Reality: Standard apps don't know what to do with a bowl of Laksa, a plate of Nasi Lemak, or custom Economic Rice (Cai Fan) selections. Local food often hides massive carbohydrate loads and high sodium.
* What Makanova Does: Uses OpenAI's gpt-4o image scanner to break down local hawker foods. It calculates glycemic load adjustments for Asian bodies and provides a Smart Hawker Swap engine to guide users to healthier options at the exact same location.

 ☀️ Pillar 3: Asian Climates (The Weather Adapter)

* The Reality: Singapore has high heat and intense relative humidity (often over 85%). This stops sweat from evaporating, causing heat stress and exhaustion even during night runs.
* What Makanova Does: Features a Tropical Climate Monitor. It scans local weather indicators (like the Wet Bulb Globe Temperature) and tells the user exactly when it is safe to exercise outside versus when to work out indoors, alongside personalized hydration alerts (e.g., tracking a target of 300ml of water per hour of exposure).

 💼 Pillar 4: Asian Work Cultures (The Lifestyle Tracker)

* The Reality: Students, interns, and early-career workers deal with long sitting hours, intense late-night desk work ("mugging"), high stress, and constant reliance on local coffee (*Kopi*) to stay awake.
* What Makanova Does: Features an Hourly Desk-Inactivity Alert and point tracking system. It reminds users to stand up and stretch to combat physical inactivity, tracks local caffeine intake boundaries, and offers quick micro-recovery routines to prevent burnout.
